# Fever

Fever is a body temperature higher than normal (often \(\ge 38^\circ C / 100.4^\circ F\)). Many viral and bacterial infections can cause fever.

## What you can do now (general)
- Rest and drink fluids.
- Consider checking temperature periodically.
- If you use fever reducers, follow the label and avoid exceeding daily maximums.

## Seek urgent care now if
- Trouble breathing, chest pain, severe confusion, fainting, or a stiff neck.
- Fever with a new rash that spreads quickly.
- Immunocompromised, pregnant, or very young infants (special risk).

## Follow-up questions to refine triage
- How high is the temperature and how was it measured?
- How many days has it been present?
- Any red flags: chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache, stiff neck?
