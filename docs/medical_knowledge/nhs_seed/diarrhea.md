# Diarrhoea

## Overview
Loose frequent stools are common with infections, food intolerances, and medication effects. Hydration is the priority for most adults with mild illness.

## What you can do now
- Oral rehydration solutions or diluted clear fluids if tolerated.
- Small frequent sips if nauseated.
- Simple foods as appetite returns.

## Red flags
- Signs of severe dehydration: confusion, very little urine, dizziness on standing.
- Blood or black stools, high fever, severe abdominal pain.
- Recent antibiotic use with profuse watery diarrhoea — seek advice promptly.

## When to seek medical advice
- Symptoms beyond seven days, or unable to keep fluids down for 12–24 hours.
- Age over 65, pregnancy, or significant heart or kidney disease with ongoing losses.
