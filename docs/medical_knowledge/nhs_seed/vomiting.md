# Vomiting

## Overview
Vomiting can accompany infections, pregnancy, migraine, medications, and other conditions. The main risk is dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

## What you can do now
- Frequent small sips of fluid; pause if vomiting restarts, then retry slowly.
- Rest the stomach: avoid large meals until settled.
- Seek local advice if pregnancy-related sickness is severe.

## Red flags
- Vomit with blood or coffee-ground appearance.
- Severe headache, stiff neck, confusion, or severe abdominal pain.
- Signs of dehydration or inability to keep any fluids down for many hours.

## When to seek medical advice
- Vomiting lasting more than 48 hours without improvement.
- Projectile vomiting in an infant, or weight loss from recurrent vomiting.
