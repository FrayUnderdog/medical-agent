# Headache

## Overview
Headaches have many causes, from tension and dehydration to migraine and, rarely, serious conditions. Patterns and associated symptoms matter when deciding how quickly to seek help.

## What you can do now
- Rest in a quiet, dim room if light or sound worsens pain.
- Hydration and regular meals.
- Simple analgesics only as directed on the label if suitable for you.

## Red flags
- A sudden “thunderclap” worst-ever headache.
- Headache with fever and stiff neck, new weakness, confusion, or after head injury.
- Headache with visual loss, speech problems, or one-sided numbness.

## When to seek medical advice
- New headache after age 50, progressive pattern, or headaches triggered by exertion or position.
- Headaches frequent enough to interfere with life most weeks.
