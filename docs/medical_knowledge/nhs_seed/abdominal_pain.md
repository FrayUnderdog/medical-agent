# Abdominal pain

## Overview
Belly pain ranges from indigestion and gastroenteritis to problems needing urgent care. Location, timing, and associated symptoms guide urgency.

## What you can do now
- Sip fluids if nausea is mild and you can keep them down.
- Avoid alcohol and very fatty foods while unsettled.
- Rest and monitor pain pattern.

## Red flags
- Rigid abdomen, fainting, or severe sudden pain.
- Blood in vomit or black tarry stools.
- Severe testicular pain, shoulder-tip pain with dizziness, or pregnancy with severe pain or bleeding.

## When to seek medical advice
- Localised pain worsening over hours, persistent vomiting, or fever.
- Unexplained weight loss or pain affecting eating for weeks.
