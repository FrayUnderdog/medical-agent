# Minor cuts and grazes

## Overview
Small superficial wounds usually heal with cleaning and protection. Depth, contamination, and tetanus status influence care.

## What you can do now
- Wash hands; rinse wound under clean running water.
- Gentle cleaning around the area; pat dry.
- Cover with sterile dressing; change if wet or dirty.

## Red flags
- Deep gaping wounds, embedded objects, or uncontrolled bleeding.
- Animal or human bites needing assessment.
- Spreading redness, pus, fever, or red streaks up the limb.

## When to seek medical advice
- Wound edges not closing, or foreign body sensation.
- Tetanus vaccination status uncertain for dirty wounds — seek advice.
