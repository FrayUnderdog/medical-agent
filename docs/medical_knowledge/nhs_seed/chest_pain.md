# Chest pain — safety first

## Overview
Chest pain has many causes, including muscle strain and reflux, but also heart and lung emergencies. When in doubt, seek urgent assessment.

## What you can do now
- If pain is severe, crushing, spreads to the arm, jaw, or back, or comes with breathlessness, sweat, or nausea — call emergency services now.
- Stop exertion; sit and try to stay calm while help is arranged.

## Red flags
- Pressure-like pain with exertion, faintness, or palpitations.
- Sudden sharp pain with breathlessness.
- Pain with new confusion or blue lips.

## When to seek medical advice
- Any new unexplained chest pain should be reviewed, even if mild.
- Pain after minor trauma that persists or worsens.
