# Wrist sprain

## Overview
Falls onto an outstretched hand can sprain wrist ligaments or hide a fracture. Persistent focal tenderness needs assessment.

## What you can do now
- Rest from painful loading; splint or support if advised over the counter.
- Ice and elevation early.
- Gentle range of motion as pain allows after a few days.

## Red flags
- Obvious deformity, open wound, or numb cold fingers.
- Snuffbox tenderness after fall — clinician assessment for scaphoid injury.

## When to seek medical advice
- Pain not improving after 10–14 days.
- Clicking with instability affecting function.
