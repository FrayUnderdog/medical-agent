# Period pain (dysmenorrhoea)

## Overview
Cramping with periods is common. Primary dysmenorrhoea often improves with simple measures; new severe pain may need evaluation.

## What you can do now
- Heat pack on lower abdomen or back.
- Gentle movement, stretching, or yoga if tolerated.
- Over-the-counter pain relief per label if suitable for you.

## Red flags
- Soaking pads hourly, large clots with dizziness.
- Fever with pelvic pain.
- Pain with fainting.

## When to seek medical advice
- New pain after years of mild periods.
- Pain with intercourse, abnormal discharge, or difficulty conceiving.
