# Insect bites and stings

## Overview
Most bites cause local redness and itch. Stingers should be removed if visible; local swelling can look dramatic but still be benign.

## What you can do now
- Wash the area; cold pack for swelling.
- Elevate a swollen limb if comfortable.
- Observe for spreading warmth beyond the first day.

## Red flags
- Throat tightness, wheeze, widespread hives, or faintness after a sting.
- Rapidly spreading hot redness with fever (possible serious infection).

## When to seek medical advice
- Facial or mouth bites with increasing swelling.
- Large swelling not improving after several days.
