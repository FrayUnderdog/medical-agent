# Ankle sprain

## Overview
Twisting the ankle can stretch or tear ligaments. Pain and swelling are expected; many people improve with protection, relative rest, ice, compression, and elevation in the early phase.

## What you can do now
- Protect weight-bearing: use support as needed and avoid painful activities.
- Ice 15–20 minutes several times daily for the first 48–72 hours if helpful.
- Compression bandage if comfortable, not so tight as to cause numbness.
- Elevate the ankle when resting.

## Red flags
- Obvious deformity, inability to take four steps, or focal bone tenderness suggesting fracture.
- Numb blue toes, severe pain out of proportion, or calf swelling with breathlessness (possible clot — urgent).

## When to seek medical advice
- No improvement after one to two weeks or recurrent instability giving way.
- Pain higher in the leg after trauma.
