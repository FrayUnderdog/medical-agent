# Low mood

## Overview
Feeling down for days or weeks can happen with stress, sleep loss, and life events. Persistent low mood may be depression and deserves support.

## What you can do now
- Maintain basic routines: meals, hygiene, daylight exposure.
- Brief walks even when motivation is low.
- Talk to someone trusted; reduce alcohol which worsens the mood and sleep cycle.

## Red flags
- Thoughts of suicide or self-harm — urgent help.
- Psychosis symptoms (for example hearing voices commanding harm) — urgent help.

## When to seek medical advice
- Mood low most of the day for two weeks with poor sleep and concentration.
- Mood swings with high-energy risky periods — clinician assessment.
