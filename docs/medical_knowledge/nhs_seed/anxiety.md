# Anxiety

## Overview
Anxiety involves worry and physical tension that can interfere with sleep and concentration. It is common and treatable with support and skills.

## What you can do now
- Regular sleep schedule and reduced late caffeine.
- Brief daily walks or movement you enjoy.
- Grounding: slow breathing, naming things you see, gentle muscle relaxation.

## Red flags
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide — seek immediate help from local crisis services.
- Panic symptoms with chest pain you cannot distinguish from cardiac pain — err on urgent assessment if new.

## When to seek medical advice
- Anxiety dominating most days for weeks.
- Avoidance shrinking your life (work, travel, social contact).
