# Allergic reaction (mild to moderate)

## Overview
Allergic symptoms can be skin-only or involve breathing and circulation in severe cases. This page focuses on general education, not individual diagnosis.

## What you can do now
- Stop eating or using the suspected trigger if safe to do so.
- Cool compresses for localised itch or swelling.
- Monitor breathing, voice changes, and swallowing closely.

## Red flags
- Any airway symptoms, widespread hives with dizziness, or feeling faint.
- Swelling of tongue or throat, or wheeze.
- Symptoms progressing over minutes — err on the side of urgent care.

## When to seek medical advice
- Recurrent reactions where the trigger is unclear — ask for allergy clinic referral.
- Mild symptoms that keep returning with certain foods or drugs.
