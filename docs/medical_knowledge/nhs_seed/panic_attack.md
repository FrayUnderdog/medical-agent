# Panic attacks

## Overview
Panic attacks are intense surges of fear with bodily symptoms that peak within minutes. They are alarming but not usually physically dangerous in otherwise healthy people.

## What you can do now
- Remind yourself the peak often passes within 10–20 minutes.
- Slow exhale-longer-than-inhale breathing if safe for you.
- Sit down to reduce injury if lightheaded.

## Red flags
- First episode with chest pain radiating or exertional pattern — urgent assessment to rule out cardiac causes.
- Fainting with exercise.

## When to seek medical advice
- Frequent attacks affecting daily life.
- Driving avoidance or agoraphobia developing after attacks.
