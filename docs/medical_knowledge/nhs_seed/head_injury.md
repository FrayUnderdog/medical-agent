# Head injury (minor to moderate concern)

## Overview
Head bumps range from trivial scalp bruises to concussions and skull injuries. Risk depends on mechanism, age, blood thinners, and symptoms.

## What you can do now
- Observe for vomiting, worsening headache, confusion, or drowsiness after a bump.
- Rest and avoid alcohol for 24–48 hours after mild concussion-type symptoms.
- Gradual return to activity per clinician guidance if concussion is diagnosed.

## Red flags
- Repeated vomiting, seizure, unequal pupils, weakness, or slurred speech.
- High-energy trauma, fall from height, or assault with loss of consciousness.
- Age over 65 on blood thinners with head impact — low threshold for assessment.

## When to seek medical advice
- Symptoms persisting or worsening over 24–48 hours.
- Clear fluid from nose or ears after head injury.
