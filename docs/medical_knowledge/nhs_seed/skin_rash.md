# Skin rash

## Overview
Rashes have many causes: infection, allergy, irritation, and chronic skin disease. Itching, pain, speed of spread, and mucosal involvement matter for urgency.

## What you can do now
- Avoid scratching; cool compresses may ease itch.
- Fragrance-free emollients for dry irritated skin.
- Note new medicines or foods in the prior days.

## Red flags
- Rash with fever and rapidly spreading purple spots.
- Swelling of lips or tongue, wheeze, or faintness with rash.
- Painful blistering rash near the eye.

## When to seek medical advice
- Rash involving eyes, mouth, or genitals.
- Rash not improving after a reasonable trial of gentle care, or widespread hives recurring.
