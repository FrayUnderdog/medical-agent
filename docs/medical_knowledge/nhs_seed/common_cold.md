# Common cold

## Overview
Colds are usually mild viral infections of the nose and throat. Symptoms often peak in the first few days and then improve over one to two weeks. Most people recover with rest and simple measures.

## What you can do now
- Rest, fluids, and simple comfort measures.
- Saline nasal rinses or steamy showers may ease congestion.
- Throat lozenges or warm drinks can soothe irritation (if safe for you).

## Red flags
- Trouble breathing, chest pain, confusion, or severe weakness.
- Symptoms that are dramatically worse than a usual cold for you.

## When to seek medical advice
- Symptoms beyond about 10–14 days without improvement.
- Ear pain, severe sinus pain, or high fever that concerns you.
