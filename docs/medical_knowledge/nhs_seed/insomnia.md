# Insomnia

## Overview
Trouble falling or staying asleep is common. Sleep hygiene helps many; chronic insomnia may need structured therapy.

## What you can do now
- Fixed wake time daily; light morning daylight.
- Wind-down hour without bright screens if possible.
- Cool, dark, quiet bedroom; reserve bed mainly for sleep.

## Red flags
- Sleepiness causing unsafe driving or work near machinery.
- Loud snoring with choking pauses (possible sleep apnoea).

## When to seek medical advice
- Insomnia most nights for over a month affecting daytime function.
- Restless legs disturbing sleep nightly.
