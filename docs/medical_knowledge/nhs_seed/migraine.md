# Migraine

## Overview
Migraine often features throbbing head pain with nausea and sensitivity to light or sound. Attacks vary in length and disability. This page is general education only.

## What you can do now
- Rest early in an attack; reduce sensory triggers.
- Consistent sleep, meals, and hydration day to day.
- Keep a simple symptom diary to discuss with a clinician.

## Red flags
- Sudden severe headache unlike your usual pattern.
- Neurological deficits that do not resolve as expected.
- Headache with fever and stiff neck.

## When to seek medical advice
- Attacks very frequent, disabling, or changing character.
- You need acute treatments more than a few days per month — review with a clinician.
