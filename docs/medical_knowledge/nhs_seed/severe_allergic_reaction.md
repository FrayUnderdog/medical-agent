# Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis awareness)

## Overview
Severe allergy can affect breathing and blood pressure quickly. Early adrenaline where prescribed can be life-saving — training matters.

## What you can do now
- Lie with legs elevated if faint but breathing is okay — follow local emergency instructions.
- If you carry prescribed adrenaline, use it when indicated in your action plan.
- Call emergency services for airway symptoms, widespread hives with faintness, or rapid progression.

## Red flags
- Any throat or tongue swelling with breathing difficulty.
- Collapse or unresponsiveness.

## When to seek medical advice
- Known severe allergy should have a written emergency plan from a clinician.
