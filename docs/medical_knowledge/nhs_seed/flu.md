# Flu (influenza-like illness)

## Overview
Influenza-like illness can start suddenly with aches, fatigue, fever, and respiratory symptoms. Severity varies widely. This information is educational and not a substitute for professional care.

## What you can do now
- Rest at home while you recover if you are otherwise low risk and stable.
- Fluids and light meals as tolerated.
- Reduce spread: wash hands, cover coughs, consider staying home while febrile.

## Red flags
- Shortness of breath at rest, chest pain, confusion, or inability to keep fluids down.
- Coughing up blood, severe dehydration, or fainting.

## When to seek medical advice
- You are pregnant, elderly, have chronic heart or lung disease, or feel worse after initial improvement.
- Symptoms not improving after about a week or any new focal symptoms (ear, chest, sinuses).
